      # Summary
      The Titanic Dataset gives an insight into the number of people that survived the disaster with 0 (Not Survived) and 1 (Survived). Out of the total passengers that were on board the ship, less percentage of people surived where as majority of them could not survive the disaster.
      Q1 Distributions of passengers who survived based on age shows that children survived more as compared to people above 35 years of age. For people with age between 18 and 35 years, ratio of survival and death is almost same. We could say that preference was given to save children first (based on age).
      Q2 Distributions of passengers who survived based on class shows that people in class 1 survived more as compared to people in class 3.
      The people travelling in each class was not the same. For this purpose, we can continue the exploration by taking the mean of values according to the class so that we can get a proper distribution of the same.
      Q3 Distribution of passengers with respect to survival based on sex shows that female passengers were given preference over male passengers.
